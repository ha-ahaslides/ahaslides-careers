1. Teamwork

Respect team routines, align on the “why” and priorities, and actively support others to remove blockers.

What does it mean?

  • Follow team routines and agreements.
  • Align on the “why” before jumping into execution. Before starting work, make sure you understand the goal, priorities, and trade-offs. If unclear, pause and clarify.
  • Re-align quickly when things change. When new information, constraints, or decisions appear, communicate early and help the team adjust course together.
  • Actively support others and remove blockers.
  • Optimise for team outcomes, not individual wins.

2. Disciplined Ownership (Tự Giác)

Take initiative, use freedom responsibly, and own outcomes without waiting to be asked.

What does it mean?

  • Manage your time, priorities, commitments, and resources with self-discipline.
  • Speak up early when something needs attention. Never wait to be asked or assume someone else will handle it.
  • Use your freedom and privileges responsibly.
  • Take full ownership of outcomes. Own results, not just tasks or effort. Learn from mistakes. Don’t blame.
  • If you think something needs to happen, you have a duty to address it. Don’t just complain or be indifferent.

3. Feedback-Seeking

Measure impact, invite feedback, and continuously improve through honest reflection.

What does it mean?

  • Seek feedback early, often, and obsessively - from our users, customers, stakeholders, and teammates.
  • Treat feedback as input for improvement, not as personal criticism. Make it safe for others to give you feedback.
  • Measure the impact of your work, not just activity or effort.
  • Continuously reflect, learn, and adjust how you work. Embrace mistakes. Treat problems as opportunities to improve. Never blame.
  • Give feedback constructively and timely.

4. Being Healthy, Caring Humans

Take care of your health, energy, and passions beyond work. Treat each other with kindness - no toxicity, no politics.

What does it mean?

  • Rest properly and use your time off. Keep passions and a life beyond work.
  • Make space for interests, family, and personal goals outside your job - energy and creativity come from a full life.
  • Be kind to each other. Maintain zero tolerance for toxicity and politics.
  • No gossip, personal attacks, passive aggression, or power games. Raise issues openly and constructively.

5. Global Mindset

Work confidently in English. Learn about other cultures while valuing our own cultural roots.

What does it mean?

  • Use English clearly and confidently as our shared working language.
  • Stay curious and actively learn about other cultures.
  • Show cultural sensitivity and mutual respect in interactions.
  • Be mindful of tone and humour, especially in written communication.
  • Learn from global perspectives without copying any single culture or treating one way of working as the default.
